Henry "Hank" Lee "HL" Foster Jr.

1926 -

Henry "Hank" Lee "HL" Foster Jr. was born January 1st, 1926 in Hamby, Texas to Henry Lee Foster Sr. and Lela Mae Richardson [1]. Henry Lee Foster Jr was called HL as a child, his original birth certificate actually listed his name as HL, he had it changed when joining the navy as they wouldn't accept HL as a name. On both sides of his family going by the first two initials was a traditional naming scheme, everyone knew him as HL. HL's father worked a variety of odd jobs, mostly in oil field work, construction, and general labor and as a child HL moved around central Texas quite often. Some of the places he lived as a boy included Lamesa, Odessa, Lubbock, and Hamby. Hamby was home to his maternal family and Elmdale, a small community a few miles away was home to his paternal family.

As a young boy HL was close with both sides of his family, he has kept in contact with all his relatives even to current day. He had a special relationship with his uncle, TC Richardson Jr, who was close in age to him and also his best friend. To HL TC held many roles including a brother and a paternal figure in his life, the two would for their entire lives.

HL graduated high school in 1943 with a 92.54 average, immediately after he joined the US Navy to participate in the war effort. After he graduated he attended basic and went to the University of Idaho to attend their radio school program. At Radio School he was given high praise, he was claimed to be "One of the best students in the history of this school" in one report given to his parents. He recieved a perfect score in radio procedure which at the time was the only perfect score ever given by the University. He averaged 100% in touch typing, 92.5% in radio code, 100% in radio procedure, and 95.8% in radio theory, averaging 97.1% for his 850 hours of class work. Physically he was rated first in his company and second in his regiment, he was six foot one and weighed 210 lbs.

After graduating Radio School he shipped off on the U.S.S Cyrene on October 11th, 1944 as a Seaman First Class. At this time he was a seaman first class. On January 26th, 1945 he was transfered to LST 334, which he would land on Okinawa in on April 2nd, 1945. The day before the Battle of Okinawa HL was promoted from seaman first class to Petty Officer Third Class with the rating of Radioman. On May 14th, 1945 he was transfered from LST 334 to LST 476, a ship that he would stay on for the majority of the rest of his time in Service. By May 1st, 1945 he was promoted to Coxswain of the U.S.S Cyrene, which he had been transfered back to sometime after April 1945. On November 1st, 1945 he was promoted to Petty Officer Second Class with a rating of Radioman while on board LST 476. On Feb 12, 1946 HL was transfered for temporary duty to LST 672 before being transfered again to LST 911 on Feb 16, a few days later.
